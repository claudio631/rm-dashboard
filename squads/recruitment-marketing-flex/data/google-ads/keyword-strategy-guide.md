# Google Ads Keyword Strategy Guide

> Source: [Google Business Resource Hub](https://business.google.com/us/resources/)

## Keyword Research Process

### Step 1: Seed Keyword Generation
Start with core business terms and expand:
- Job titles: "warehouse worker", "delivery driver", "caregiver"
- Location modifiers: "jobs in [city]", "[role] near me"
- Intent signals: "hiring now", "apply today", "temp work"
- Industry terms: "staffing agency", "flexible shifts", "same day pay"

### Step 2: Use Keyword Planner
Google's free tool provides:
- Search volume estimates per keyword
- Competition levels (low/medium/high)
- Suggested bid ranges
- Related keyword ideas
- Seasonal trends and forecasts

### Step 3: Evaluate and Select
| Criteria | What to Look For |
|----------|-----------------|
| Relevance | Directly matches job roles you're hiring for |
| Volume | Enough monthly searches to drive meaningful traffic |
| Competition | Balance between reach and cost |
| Intent | High commercial intent ("apply", "hiring", "jobs") |
| Cost | CPC within your target CPA threshold |

## Match Types

### Broad Match
- **How it works:** Shows ads for searches related to your keyword meaning, including synonyms, related topics, and variations
- **Example:** Keyword `warehouse jobs` may show for "logistics employment", "fulfillment center hiring"
- **Best for:** Smart Bidding campaigns; gives AI maximum signal access
- **Key stat:** Upgrading exact match to broad match + Smart Bidding yields **+35% more conversions** on average
- **Recommendation:** Google's default and recommended match type when using Smart Bidding

### Phrase Match
- **How it works:** Shows ads for searches that include the meaning of your keyword
- **Example:** Keyword `"warehouse jobs"` may show for "warehouse jobs near me", "best warehouse jobs in Dallas"
- **Best for:** Moderate control when you need keyword meaning preserved
- **Use when:** You have specific phrasing requirements or sensitive verticals

### Exact Match
- **How it works:** Shows ads for searches with the same meaning or intent as your keyword
- **Example:** Keyword `[warehouse jobs]` shows for "warehouse employment", "warehouse job openings"
- **Best for:** Maximum precision targeting; limited budgets
- **Use when:** You need tight control over which searches trigger ads

### Match Type Selection Matrix

| Scenario | Recommended Match | Why |
|----------|------------------|-----|
| Smart Bidding active | Broad | AI needs full signal access for optimization |
| Limited budget, known terms | Exact | Prevents spend on tangential searches |
| Testing new markets | Broad | Discovers candidate search patterns |
| High-competition keywords | Phrase/Exact | Controls costs on expensive terms |
| Brand keywords | Exact | Prevents broad matching to competitors |

## Negative Keywords

### Purpose
Exclude irrelevant searches to conserve budget and improve ad relevance.

### Essential Negative Keywords for Recruitment
```
# Irrelevant intent
- "salary" (info seekers, not applicants)
- "reviews" (employer review seekers)
- "interview questions" (prep, not applying)
- "resume template" (not ready to apply)

# Wrong job types (if not offered)
- "remote" (if only on-site roles)
- "full time" (if only temp/flex)
- "internship" (if not applicable)

# Competitor brands (unless targeting)
- [competitor names]

# Geographic exclusions
- [cities where not hiring]
```

### Negative Keyword Management
1. Review Search Terms Report weekly
2. Add irrelevant terms as negatives
3. Use negative keyword lists shared across campaigns
4. Apply at campaign or ad group level as appropriate
5. Monitor for over-blocking (don't exclude valid variations)

## Long-Tail Keyword Strategy

### Why Long-Tail for Recruitment
- Lower competition and CPC
- Higher intent (more specific = more ready to apply)
- Better conversion rates
- Examples:
  - "warehouse jobs $18 hour Dallas" vs "warehouse jobs"
  - "flexible temp work no experience needed" vs "temp work"
  - "night shift forklift operator hiring" vs "forklift operator"

### Building Long-Tail Keywords
```
[job title] + [location] + [qualifier]
  └── "delivery driver" + "Houston" + "hiring now"
  └── "caregiver" + "near me" + "flexible hours"
  └── "warehouse" + "Los Angeles" + "same day pay"
```

## Keyword Organization

### Campaign Structure
```
Campaign: Warehouse Roles — Texas
├── Ad Group: General Warehouse
│   ├── warehouse jobs texas
│   ├── warehouse worker hiring
│   └── warehouse employment near me
├── Ad Group: Forklift Operators
│   ├── forklift operator jobs
│   ├── forklift driver hiring
│   └── certified forklift jobs
└── Ad Group: Warehouse Night Shift
    ├── night shift warehouse jobs
    ├── overnight warehouse work
    └── warehouse evening shift hiring
```

### Grouping Rules
1. One theme per ad group (3-20 keywords)
2. Group by job role, not by match type
3. Mirror website/landing page structure
4. Create separate ad copy per ad group theme
5. Don't reorganize after accumulating data (resets learnings)

## Keyword Performance Metrics

| Metric | Target | Action if Below |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| CTR | >3% (Search) | Improve ad relevance, tighten match types |
| Quality Score | ≥7/10 | Improve landing page, ad copy alignment |
| Conversion Rate | >5% (recruitment) | Check landing page UX, form length |
| CPA | Within target | Adjust bids, pause low performers |
| Impression Share | >70% (brand) | Increase budget or bids |
