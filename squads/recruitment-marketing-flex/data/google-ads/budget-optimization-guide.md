# Google Ads Budget Optimization Guide

> Source: [Google Business Resource Hub](https://business.google.com/us/resources/)

## 10 Budget Management Principles

### 1. Use PPC for Cost Control
Google Ads operates on pay-per-click — you only pay when someone clicks your ad. This puts you in direct control of spend. Set maximum CPC bids per keyword to cap what you're willing to pay.

### 2. Set Goals Before Budget
Define what success looks like before allocating budget:
| Goal | Metric | Budget Approach |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| Drive applications | Cost per Application (CPA) | Work backward from target CPA × volume |
| Build awareness | Impressions, Reach | CPM-based budget |
| Drive site traffic | Clicks, CTR | CPC-based budget |
| Phone inquiries | Cost per Call | Call-focused bidding |

### 3. Link Analytics to Ads
Connect Google Ads and Google Analytics 4 to understand post-click behavior:
- Which clicks convert to applications?
- Where do candidates drop off?
- Which campaigns drive quality traffic?
- What's the true cost per qualified applicant?

### 4. Maintain Budget Discipline
- Set a **monthly budget ceiling** and don't exceed it
- Google averages daily spending across the month (may spend up to 2x daily budget on high-demand days)
- Allow **2-4 weeks** of data before making budget changes
- Avoid reactive budget cuts based on 1-2 days of data

### 5. Prioritize Search Over Display Initially
| Channel | Avg. Conversion Rate | When to Use |
|---------|---------------------|-------------|
| Search | 3-8% | Always (primary) |
| Display | 0.5-2% | After Search is optimized |
| YouTube | 1-3% | Brand awareness goals |
| Performance Max | Varies | Cross-channel expansion |

Start with Search campaigns where candidate intent is highest. Expand to Display/YouTube after establishing Search baselines.

### 6. Use Geographic Targeting
- Show ads only in markets with active hiring needs
- Test individual cities before expanding regionally
- Adjust bids by location based on application rates
- Exclude areas where you don't have open positions

### 7. Bid for Lower Positions When Budget-Constrained
- Positions 3-5 often have lower CPC with decent conversion rates
- Candidates searching carefully (scrolling past top results) may be higher quality
- Use Target Impression Share with a max CPC cap for controlled visibility

### 8. Prioritize Long-Tail Keywords
| Keyword Type | Avg. CPC | Conversion Rate | Volume |
|-------------|----------|-----------------|--------|
| Head terms ("jobs") | High | Low | Very High |
| Mid-tail ("warehouse jobs") | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Long-tail ("warehouse jobs $18 hr Dallas") | Low | High | Low |

Long-tail keywords (3+ words) are cheaper and convert better because they capture specific intent.

### 9. Create Multiple Focused Campaigns
Instead of one generic campaign, create focused campaigns:
```
Campaign: Warehouse — Texas (Budget: $50/day)
Campaign: Delivery — California (Budget: $75/day)
Campaign: Caregiver — Florida (Budget: $40/day)
Campaign: Brand — National (Budget: $30/day)
```
Benefits: targeted budgets, relevant landing pages, clearer performance data.

### 10. Monitor and Adjust Continuously
Weekly budget review checklist:
- [ ] Check spend vs. budget pacing
- [ ] Identify top-performing campaigns (increase budget)
- [ ] Identify underperformers (reduce budget or pause)
- [ ] Review cost per conversion trends
- [ ] Reallocate from low-CPA to high-CPA campaigns
- [ ] Check for wasted spend (irrelevant search terms)

## Smart Bidding Strategies

| Strategy | Best For | How It Works |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Maximize Conversions | Volume focus | AI spends full budget to get most conversions |
| Target CPA | Cost control | AI targets your specified cost per conversion |
| Maximize Conversion Value | Revenue focus | AI prioritizes high-value conversions |
| Target ROAS | ROI focus | AI targets your return on ad spend |

### Smart Bidding Best Practices
- Need 15+ conversions in 30 days for reliable optimization
- Start with Maximize Conversions → switch to Target CPA once baseline established
- Value-based bidding (Target ROAS) yields **+14% more conversion value** at similar ROAS
- Don't change bid strategy during first 2 weeks (learning period)

## Budget Allocation Framework

### Monthly Budget Distribution (Recruitment)
```
Total Monthly Budget: $X

Search Campaigns:          60-70% ($X × 0.65)
├── High-priority markets: 40%
├── Growth markets:        15%
└── Brand defense:         10%

Performance Max:           15-20% ($X × 0.18)
├── Cross-channel reach

Display/Remarketing:       10-15% ($X × 0.12)
├── Site visitor remarketing
└── Awareness campaigns

YouTube/Video:             5-10% ($X × 0.05)
├── Employer brand
└── Hiring event promotion
```

### Budget Pacing Rules
1. Check pacing weekly (monthly spend / days elapsed × days in month)
2. If underspending: check impression share, expand keywords, or raise bids
3. If overspending: add negatives, lower bids, tighten targeting
4. Reallocate from capped campaigns to those with headroom
5. Reserve 10% monthly contingency for opportunities (events, surges)
