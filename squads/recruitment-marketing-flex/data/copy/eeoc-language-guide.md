# EEOC Language Guide — Job Ad Copy Compliance
# For Indeed Flex recruitment marketing copy
# Last Updated: 2026-04-02

---

## Overview

The Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) prohibits job advertisements that
express a preference for or discourage candidates based on:

- Age
- Race / Color / National Origin
- Sex / Gender / Gender Identity
- Religion
- Disability
- Pregnancy
- Genetic information

**Risk:** Non-compliant ads can result in EEOC complaints, discrimination lawsuits,
platform policy violations (Indeed, Meta, Google all have their own enforcement), and
reputational damage for Indeed Flex clients.

---

## Age

### Prohibited Terms
| Prohibited | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| "Young professional" | Implies age preference |
| "Recent graduate" | Implies young age |
| "Digital native" | Age stereotype |
| "Energetic young team" | Age + energy stereotype |
| "Entry-level for new grads" | Implies age |
| "Retire after long career? Try flex!" | Targets older workers — can be discriminatory framing |

### Compliant Alternatives
| Instead of... | Use... |
|--------------|--------|
| "Young, energetic team" | "High-energy team" |
| "Recent grads welcome" | "No experience needed" or "All backgrounds welcome" |
| "Entry-level for new grads" | "Entry-level position" |

---

## Gender

### Prohibited Terms
| Prohibited | Compliant Alternative |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Waitress | Server |
| Waiter | Server |
| Hostess | Host |
| Busboy / Busgirl | Busser |
| Stewardess | Flight Attendant |
| Handyman | Maintenance Worker |
| Foreman | Supervisor / Crew Lead |
| Manpower | Workforce / Team |
| "He or she must be able to..." | "The candidate must be able to..." |
| "Looking for guys who can lift..." | "Must be able to lift [X] lbs" |

### Pronouns in Copy
- Use "you" and "your" — second person avoids gender entirely
- Never use "he", "she", or "they" to describe the worker in a job ad
- ✅ "You'll be responsible for..."
- ❌ "He/she will be responsible for..."

---

## National Origin / Race

### Prohibited
- Requiring specific languages unless genuinely required for the job
- Phrases that imply preference for (or against) workers of a specific background
- Targeting ads exclusively to one ethnic group when other groups are qualified

### Compliant Bilingual Copy
- Running Spanish-language ads is **legal and recommended** — it expands reach
- Spanish ads must target language preference, not ethnicity
- Job requirements for Spanish fluency are only compliant if the job genuinely requires it
- Example: ✅ "Bilingual (English/Spanish) preferred for client communication" — OK if true
- Example: ❌ "Must be Hispanic to understand our culture" — NOT OK

---

## Disability

### Prohibited Framing
- Requiring candidates to disclose medical conditions in the job ad
- Implying only fully able-bodied candidates qualify without a bona fide occupational reason
- Language like "must be physically perfect" or "no health issues"

### Compliant Physical Requirements
Always state actual job requirements in objective, functional terms:

| ✅ Compliant | ❌ Non-Compliant |
|------------|----------------|
| "Must be able to lift up to 50 lbs repeatedly" | "Must be physically strong and healthy" |
| "Ability to stand for 8-hour shifts" | "No physical limitations" |
| "Must be able to work in hot kitchen environments" | "Must be in excellent health" |

---

## Religion

- Do not reference religious holidays or schedules in a way that implies preference
- ✅ "Flexible schedule available" — neutral
- ❌ "Must be available Sundays (church excused)" — implies religious preference/exception

---

## Pregnancy / Family Status

| ❌ Prohibited | ✅ Compliant |
|-------------|-------------|
| "Must not be pregnant" | (simply don't mention it) |
| "No childcare conflicts" | "Must be available for assigned shifts" |
| "Looking for someone without family obligations" | "Reliable availability required" |

---

## Background Checks & Criminal History

### Fair Chance / Ban the Box
Many states and cities (including FL, TX, IL) have "ban the box" laws restricting when you
can ask about criminal history.

**Best Practice for Indeed Flex ads:**
- Do NOT include "must pass background check" in the requirements bullets
- If legally required for the role, add it in the logistics/interview section at the bottom
- Phrasing: "Selected candidates will undergo a standard background screening process"
- Never use: "No felonies", "Clean record required", "Criminal-free"

## EEOC-Safe Copy Audit Checklist

Before publishing any job ad copy, verify:

- [ ] No age-specific language (young, recent grad, digital native, etc.)
- [ ] Job title is gender-neutral (Server not Waitress, Busser not Busboy)
- [ ] No pronouns referring to the worker (use "you" or the job title)
- [ ] Physical requirements stated as functional tasks with specific measurements
- [ ] No reference to nationality, race, or ethnicity as a preference
- [ ] Language requirements only included if genuinely required for the role
- [ ] Background check language placed at the end, not in requirements bullets
- [ ] No reference to religious observance, family status, or pregnancy
- [ ] Equal Opportunity statement NOT required in ad copy (it belongs in the footer of a full job description, not in PPC ad copy)

## Platform-Specific Enforcement

| Platform | Key Rule |
|----------|---------|
| Indeed | Flags age-specific and gender-specific language automatically — may suppress listing |
| Meta | Housing, Employment, Credit (HEC) ads have restricted targeting — cannot target by age, gender, or ZIP for job ads |
| Google | Personalized advertising policies restrict targeting for employment ads |
| LinkedIn | Similar restrictions to Meta for job-related campaigns |

**Meta Employment Ad Note:** All Meta job ads must use the "Special Ad Category: Employment" setting, which disables age, gender, and ZIP targeting. Copy must be inclusive by default since targeting is broad.
